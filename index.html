5 chord qualities

  • Δ Major 7 1 3 5 7
  • 7 Dominant 7 1 3 5 b7
  • - Minor 7 1 b3 5 b7
  • ø Half-diminished 1 b3 b5 b7
  • ° Diminished 7 1 b3 b5 6

Dyas · Monk Institute

Guide tones

The 3rd and 7th carry the chord's identity. Two notes tell you everything: major vs. minor (3rd), maj7 vs. dom7 (7th). Add the 9th on top and you've got a working comping voicing.

Davey · Mt. Hood

Voice leading

In a ii-V-I: the 7th drops a half-step to become the 3rd of the next chord. The 3rd stays put to become the 7th. Two voices barely move.

Dyas · Monk Institute

Category A & B

Two one-handed voicing shapes. A: 3rd on bottom (3-7-9 for maj/dom, b3-b7-9 for minor). B: 7th on bottom (7-3-5 for maj, b7-3-6 for dom, b7-b3-5 for minor). Alternate them through a progression for smooth voice leading.

Dyas · Monk Institute

ii-V-I in major

Dm7 → G7 → Cmaj7. The most common progression in jazz. Practiced around the cycle of fifths in all 12 keys.

Both handouts

ii-V-i in minor

Dø → G7alt → Cm. The half-diminished ii is the gateway to minor tonality. The 7alt brings the b9, #9, #5 to set up the resolution.

Dyas · Monk Institute

Tune learning order

  1. Listen to the definitive recording
  2. Memorize the form
  3. Memorize root movement
  4. Memorize chord qualities, arpeggiate
  5. Play related scales in eighths
  6. Memorize the head
  7. Improvise with play-along
  8. Transcribe phrases
  9. Improvise incorporating transcribed phrases
